3. Activation Conditions for White Hole Ejection One of the persistent issues with traditional white hole theory is that they appear to be mathematically valid but physically absurd. A permanent white hole would be: constantly ejecting matter, defying thermodynamic stability, and collapsing immediately. The CBH model resolves this by framing the white hole as an intermittent feature, not a static one. 3.1. White Holes as Pressure-Triggered Events: Matter is ingested by the black hole, compressed at or near the singularity, and held until a threshold condition is met—mass, entropy density, or spacetime curvature. At that point, the “cosmic sphincter” opens. 3.2. Loop Quantum Gravity and Bounce Cosmology: Certain quantum gravity models replace the singularity with a bounce, suggesting matter rebounds and emerges as an expanding white hole. CBH frames this as a pressure- release mechanism. 3.3. Biological Analogy: Biology shows that continuous intake without output is fatal. CBH echoes this: black holes consume, compress, and eventually expel. White holes are essential to cosmic health. 3.4. The Cosmic Constipation Hypothesis: If no white hole forms, Hawking radiation becomes the fallback—slow, inefficient, and comparable to cosmic indigestion.
