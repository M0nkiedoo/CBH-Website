The Anatomy of Spacetime Digestion

In the CBH Model, the dynamics of black holes and their hypothetical white hole counterparts are mapped onto the functional anatomy of a digestive tract. While the analogy is playful, it offers a surprisingly cohesive model of input, transformation, storage, pressure buildup, and eventual expulsion.

The Black Hole — The Mouth:

Like the beginning of a digestive system, the black hole serves as the intake port. Matter, energy, light, and information are pulled across the event horizon, never to return. The event horizon functions like a sphincter or one-way valve, allowing inflow but preventing any exit.

The Singularity — The Stomach:

The singularity represents the most extreme condition of compression, reducing matter to its most fundamental states.2.3. Hawking Radiation — The Burp: Hawking radiation provides a thermodynamic release valve. It’s weak and slow—like gas escaping over time.

The White Hole — The Anus:

When enough gravitational “mass-pressure” builds, a white hole event emerges, expelling restructured matter in an explosive, time-reversed flow.

The Wormhole Hypothesis — The Intestine:

In CBH terms, this is the intestine—a transit tube that processes compressed matter into a more stable, ejection-ready form.

The Information Paradox — The Fibre Question:

If white holes eject previously compressed contents, they may resolve the information paradox—not by preventing information loss, but by delaying its return until the ejection event.
