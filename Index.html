Abstract

We propose the Cosmic Bowel Hypothesis (CBH), a biologically inspired metaphor for black hole–white hole dynamics rooted in gravitational thermodynamics and quantum gravity. In this model, black holes function as the mouths of the universe—ingesting matter and information—while white holes are interpreted as transient, reactive ejection events akin to the anus of cosmic spacetime. The singularity serves as the compressive digestive core, and Hawking radiation manifests as a thermodynamic burp.

Framed as a conceptual alternative to static white hole models, CBH posits that white holes emerge only when sufficient gravitational “mass-pressure” accumulates, mimicking pressure-activated expulsion in biological systems. This mechanism offers an intuitive metaphor for resolving the black hole information paradox, positioning information as the fibre of the universe—necessary for maintaining spacetime regularity and avoiding cosmic constipation.

We explore connections to loop quantum gravity, bounce cosmology, and the possibility that our own universe was seeded by the white hole ejection of a previous black hole system. While CBH is semi-satirical, it provides a pedagogically useful lens for communicating entropy flow, cosmic recycling, and the irrepressible urge of the universe to relieve itself. If future anomalies such as sudden high-entropy ejections, primordial gamma bursts, or spontaneous spacetime "hiccups" are observed, CBH offers one explanation: The universe passed something.
